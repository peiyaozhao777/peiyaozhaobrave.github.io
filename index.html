Dissertation

Four pillars, one framework.

01
Transportation Emission Inventory

Characterizing vehicle emission patterns across 300 MSAs using road network typology and socioeconomic context.

02
Road Network Typology

Latent-class clustering of MSA road networks with OSMnx variables to reveal structural patterns linked to congestion and crash outcomes.

03
Water Violation Typology

EFA + GMM clustering of EPA SDWIS data (1991–2023) to identify non-compliant system archetypes and track temporal transitions.

04
Scenario Discovery

PRIM-based scenario discovery with Latin Hypercube Sampling and SHAP interpretation to inform IIJA infrastructure investment decisions.

The four pillars are unified by an equity-focused policy decision-making framework examining the implications of the Infrastructure Investment and Jobs Act (IIJA) on vulnerable communities — integrating transportation and water infrastructure research across spatial, temporal, and socioeconomic dimensions.
